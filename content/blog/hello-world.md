Every developer ends up building their own site sooner or later. This one is mine.

I'll post here when I have something worth saying — mostly short notes about frontend craft, Nuxt, TypeScript, performance work, and the small things I learn leading teams. I'll try to skip the think-piece takes and stick to the kind of writing I'd want to read: specific, testable, and short enough to finish on a coffee break.

## What you'll find here

- **Notes on Nuxt and Vue** — things that tripped me up, and how I fixed them.
- **Performance work** — the unglamorous stuff that actually moves LCP and CLS.
- **Leading a small frontend team** — code review habits, mentorship, and setting the bar for DX.

If any of that sounds useful, thanks for reading. If something is wrong, [email me](mailto:alikhalilll.dev@gmail.com) — I'd genuinely rather be corrected than comfortable.
